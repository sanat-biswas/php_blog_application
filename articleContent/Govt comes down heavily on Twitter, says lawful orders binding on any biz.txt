<p style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: 'open sans', Arial; font-size: 15px; background-color: #ffffff; line-height: 24px !important; padding: 2px 0px !important; margin: 0px 0px 12px !important;">The government expects&nbsp;<a class="storyTags" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #2591dc; text-decoration-line: none; outline: 0px;" href="https://www.business-standard.com/topic/twitter" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Twitter&nbsp;</a>to fully comply with its legal notice in the matter of taking down accounts that were tweeting &ldquo;ince&shy;ndiary&rdquo; hashtags related to the ongoing farmers&rsquo; protests. After a marathon meeting of top&nbsp;<a class="storyTags" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #2591dc; text-decoration-line: none; outline: 0px;" href="https://www.business-standard.com/topic/twitter" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Twitter&nbsp;</a>executives with officials in the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) on Wednesday, the government, in a strongly worded statement, said that &ldquo;lawfully passed orders are binding on any business entity&rdquo;.</p>
<p style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: 'open sans', Arial; font-size: 15px; background-color: #ffffff; line-height: 24px !important; padding: 2px 0px !important; margin: 0px 0px 12px !important;">MeitY Secretary Ajay Prakash Sawhney, while stating that&nbsp;<a class="storyTags" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #2591dc; text-decoration-line: none; outline: 0px;" href="https://www.business-standard.com/topic/twitter" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Twitter&nbsp;</a>was welcome to do business in India, expressed strong displeasure to the executives of the American firm over the way it acted after an emergency order was issued to remove the genocide- related hashtag, according to the statement. &lsquo;&rsquo;Misuse of Twitter&rsquo;s platform for execution of such campaigns designed to create disharmony and unrest in India is unacceptable and Twitter must take strong action against such well-coordinated campaigns against India,&rsquo;&rsquo; it added.</p>
<p style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: 'open sans', Arial; font-size: 15px; background-color: #ffffff; line-height: 24px !important; padding: 2px 0px !important; margin: 0px 0px 12px !important;">The Twitter leadership affirmed its commitment to follow Indian law, the statement said. Earlier, Twitter had, in a blog post, said it was exploring options under Indian law.</p>
